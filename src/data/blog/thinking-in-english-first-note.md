# Patience: The Unexpected Key to Building My First Website with AI
As a non-technical person , I recently built my first website using AI tools and web templates. What surprised me most wasn't the ease of the process, but rather a simple lesson: patience was the key to success.
## The Journey Begins: Discovering Vibe Coding
For a long time, I've wanted to build a personal website to document my thoughts and projects in the future—something I see others do effortlessly. So I decided to turn this aspiration into reality.
## First Attempt: Vibe Coding with AI Tools
I started my journey after watching a tutorial where an instructor demonstrated how to use AI tools like Trae and Cursor to build a blog using "vibe coding"—an approach that was just being popularized in 2025 when Andrej Karpathy coined the term.
The concept fascinated me: I could generate entire website code simply by providing a well-crafted prompt to an AI tool, bypassing the need to learn programming languages myself.
At first, I lacked the skills to craft an effective prompt myself. So I simply borrowed the one from the tutorial and let the AI handle the rest. What impressed me was how comprehensive the prompt was—it didn't just specify the design, but also the programming language and framework, aspects I wouldn't have known to include.
The results were remarkable. Within minutes, both AI tools generated complete, functional websites that I could immediately run locally. The entire process—downloading tools, installing them, pasting the prompt, and waiting—required surprisingly little active effort from me.
## Taking a Step Back: The Template Approach
Months later, in early 2026, I faced a decision. I could vibe code another site from scratch, but I'd heard that GitHub hosted countless beautifully designed website templates. Why rebuild what others had already perfected?
This decision led me down a different path. While I could design a rough concept and ask one AI to convert it into a prompt, then another AI to build it, the template route seemed more practical—especially since I was starting with no understanding of how to customize or deploy it.
I turned to AI for guidance on which tools and frameworks would work best.
The recommendations came back: Hugo, Astro, and similar static site
generators. I chose Astro and found the Astro Paper template—a clean,
well-maintained option.
Here's where I hit my first hurdle: GitHub was still unfamiliar territory for me. I didn't know how to search for templates or navigate the platform effectively. (I felt embarrassed admitting this.) But once I asked AI for step-by-step instructions, the process became manageable. Within minutes, I had forked the repository into my own account.
## The Deployment Challenge: When Things Go Wrong
Next, I attempted to deploy it using Zeabur. I followed every instruction, clicked deploy, and received my first error message. Undeterred, I tried investigating the logs, checking ports, and editing the package.json file.
More errors appeared.
I was stuck—until AI pointed out a critical insight: Astro sites should be deployed as static sites, but Zeabur was attempting to run them as dynamic applications using Docker. The platform simply wasn't designed for this use case.
So I switched to Vercel instead. Within moments, the deployment succeeded.
It was that simple.
This seemingly minor switch taught me a valuable lesson: platform selection matters profoundly. Different projects have different requirements, and choosing the right deployment tool from the start can save hours of troubleshooting. In the future, I'll ask AI to recommend the optimal platform before I even begin building.
Yet the deployment phase itself was emotionally taxing. I spent hours debugging without fully understanding what was happening—simply following AI's instructions and troubleshooting methodically, one step at a time.
Each error message tested my patience. After several failed attempts, I felt frustrated and seriously considered abandoning the project. But I took a short break, returned with fresh eyes, and continued. What kept me going wasn't just stubbornness—it was knowing I had no
other option but to persist. And that persistence paid off.
## From Template to Personal: Customization and Pride
Once deployed, the real work began: transforming the generic template into something uniquely mine. With AI's guidance, I located the configuration files and edited the site information, home page, and about page, gradually infusing the template with my personality and voice.
Watching the blog transform from a cookie-cutter template into something that felt authentically mine was profoundly satisfying. Yes, it's still a simple blog—but it's my blog, built entirely by me (with AI assistance).
Looking back, I'm struck by how I nearly gave up during the deployment phase. But I persevered, and now I have something tangible to show for that effort. This experience crystallized an insight I'll carry forward: 
The real blocker for non-technical people isn't intelligence or capability—**it's patience**. And with AI as your guide, patience is all
you really need.
If you're thinking about building a website but feel held back by lack of technical skills, don't. The tools exist. The tutorials exist. And now, AI can guide you through every step. What you need isn't a computer science degree—it's the willingness to sit with frustration, learn from errors, and keep going.
That's the real lesson my website taught me.
